March 29 – April 1, 2026 — latent-sae experiments

A chronological record of every experiment direction, what worked, what didn't, and where we stopped. ~50 experiments, ~$15 in Modal costs, 3 published models.

March 29 — Setup & Infrastructure

PR #1: Architecture Modernization

Starting point: fork of EleutherAI/sae with only TopK SAE. Added Gated SAE, JumpReLU SAE, k-annealing, tilted ERM, mixed precision, and cosine LR schedule. Fixed tilted ERM bug that was overwriting the entire loss, and addressed 4 code review comments (AMP unscaling, JumpReLU sparsity penalty, linear LR transformers dependency, k-annealing clamp).

Experiment Framework

Built a config-driven experiment system modeled on latent-basemap: YAML configs with SHA256 hash dedup, cartesian sweep generation, dot-notation CLI overrides. Updated train_modal.py with GPU type selection (T4/A10G/A100) and parallel .spawn() dispatch. Added experiments/run_experiment.py, compare_results.py, and 7 YAML configs.

Dead Features Bug Discovery

Between v1 and v2 sweeps, discovered three misconfigurations explaining the dead feature epidemic:

auxk_alpha=0

Dead feature revival was disabled by default. Literature uses 1/32.

dead_threshold=10M

Threshold unreachable with 10M training samples.

multi_topk unscaled

Should be L(4k)/8 per Gao et al., we added at full weight.

Expansion Factor Sweep (v2, auxk fixed)

With auxk properly enabled, swept expansion factors. The key finding of the project:

ExpansionFeaturesFVUDead %Verdict
4x1,5360.1788.1%Good

8x = same FVU as 32x, 0% dead features. Embedding SAEs need much smaller dictionaries than LLM-layer SAEs. The literature (arXiv:2408.00657) predicted this.

LISTA (Lateral Inhibition)

Implemented decoder-correlation-based lateral inhibition (from Gregor & LeCun 2010). Features suppress correlated competitors before top-k selection. Tested at three eta values.

Leaf: LISTA abandoned (decoder-correlation variant)
March 30–31 — Production Training & Hard Evaluation

Production Run: 8x k=64 (150M MiniLM)

First production-scale training: 150M embeddings (50M each from FineWeb-edu, RedPajama, Pile). FVU=0.156, 1.2% dead features, $0.99, 54 minutes on A10G.

Standard Eval (Easy Tasks)

AG News 89.0% (gap -0.9%), SST-2 80.6% (gap 0.0%), STS-B 0.860 (gap -0.021). All within the 5% threshold. But these tasks are too easy to differentiate models.

CLINC150 revealed the real gap. 150-class intent classification loses 19.6% — the SAE can't preserve fine-grained semantic distinctions at k=64.

The k Discovery: k=128 Changes Everything

Hypothesis: the bottleneck is the sparsity constraint (k=64), not dictionary size. Trained 8x k=128 and 4x k=128.

k=128 at 4x: CLINC150 gap dropped from 19.6% to 4.5%. SciFact nearly matches raw. Only 402 active features but each is highly distinct (MMCS 0.193). Half the parameters.
Leaf: 16x expansion abandoned (same as 8x but more dead)

Published All three models to enjalot/sae-all-MiniLM-L6-v2-FineWeb-RedPajama-Pile-150M

The Reduction Trap: 2x and 1x Expansion

If 4x k=128 was good, would 2x or 1x be better? Tested in autoresearch.

Better metrics but not better features. At 1x expansion with 66.7% density, the SAE is essentially learning a sparse rotation — not interpretable concepts. The "improvement" comes from less compression, not better feature quality.
Leaf: Low expansion + high k = rotation, not interpretation

March 31 — Autoresearch

Constrained Autoresearch Setup

Fixed architecture at 4x k=128 to force improvements in training procedure, not architectural shortcuts. Built Karpathy-style experiment loop: train.py (editable) + prepare.py (locked eval) + program.md (agent instructions). Fast eval: CLINC150 + SciFact + MMCS composite score in ~25 seconds.

Cosine decorrelation is the clear winner. Penalizes |cos_sim| between decoder weight vectors. Unlike squared cosine, it catches even moderate similarity. Improves both CLINC150 and SciFact.

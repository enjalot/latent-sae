Key Findings
A10G: batch_size=1024 is optimal. Peak 11,766 samp/s. Drops at 2048 due to memory bandwidth. Use batch_size=1024, grad_acc_steps=2 for effective batch of 2048. Best cost-efficiency at $2.60/100M samples.
T4: batch_size=128 is optimal. Peak 3,856 samp/s at smallest batch. The 16GB T4 saturates quickly with larger batches. T4 is both slower AND more expensive per run than A10G — skip it.
M2 Max: competitive with T4. Peak 3,317 samp/s at batch_size=2048 (86% of T4 peak). For overnight runs at $0, it's the best option for iterating on small experiments. 96GB unified memory handles large models easily.
Experiment schedule recommendation: Use M2 Max locally for quick iteration (small data, parameter tuning) — free and ~3K samp/s. Use A10G on Modal for production sweeps — $2.60/run at 100M samples, 2.4 hrs each. Skip T4 entirely. Full 7-config architecture sweep on 100M MiniLM: $18, ~2.4 hrs parallel on A10G.
